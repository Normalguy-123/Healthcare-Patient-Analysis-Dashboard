# Healthcare-Patient-Analysis-Dashboard

# Project Overview

This project analyzes hospital patient data to identify trends in patient distribution, treatment costs, recovery outcomes, and hospital department workload. The dashboard provides insights that can help healthcare administrators understand patient patterns, evaluate treatment outcomes, and make data-driven decisions for better hospital management.

The dataset contains 2,000 patient records including gender, hospital department, insurance type, treatment cost, patient outcomes, and length of hospital stay.



# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

* Total Patients: 2,000
* Total Treatment Cost: $5,108,563.44
* Average Length of Stay: 10.73 days
* Recovery Rate: 25.55%


# Business Questions

1. Which hospital department has the highest number of patients?
2. What is the distribution of patient outcomes?
3. Is there any significant difference in patient count between male and female patients?
4. Which insurance type contributes the most to total treatment cost?
5. What is the average length of hospital stay for patients?


# Dashboard Analysis

# Patient Distribution by Hospital Department

* Neurology — 427 patients
* Cardiology — 411 patients
* Pediatrics — 407 patients
* General Medicine — 384 patients
* Orthopedic — 371 patients

# Insight

Neurology recorded the highest number of patients, indicating a high demand for neurological medical services within the hospital.


# Patient Outcomes Distribution

* Improving — 25.65%
* Recovered — 25.55%
* Deceased — 24.65%
* Critical — 24.15%

 Insight

The outcomes are relatively evenly distributed across all categories, showing that patients experience a wide range of medical conditions and recovery stages.


# Patient Distribution by Gender

* Female — 1,019 patients
* Male — 981 patients

# Insight

The gender distribution among patients is nearly balanced, with a slightly higher number of female patients.


# Treatment Cost by Insurance Type

* Public Insurance — $1,716,623.06
* No Insurance — $1,710,570.04
* Private Insurance — $1,681,370.34

# Insight

Public insurance accounts for the highest total treatment cost, although uninsured patients also represent a significant portion of healthcare spending.


# Key Insights

* Neurology and cardiology departments have the highest patient volumes.
* Patient outcomes are distributed fairly evenly across recovery, improvement, critical, and deceased categories.
* Female patients slightly outnumber male patients.
* Public insurance contributes the highest treatment cost among insurance types.


# Recommendations

* Hospitals should allocate additional resources to high-demand departments such as Neurology and Cardiology.
* Healthcare providers should investigate factors affecting critical and deceased outcomes to improve patient survival rates.
* Programs may be needed to support uninsured patients, since they represent a large portion of treatment costs.
* Reducing the average length of stay (10.73 days) through improved treatment efficiency may help lower hospital costs.

* Conclusion:
  
The healthcare patient analysis dashboard provides a clear overview of patient distribution and outcomes across hospital departments. Neurology and cardiology have the highest patient counts, while orthopedic and general medicine have relatively lower volumes, indicating potential workload differences. The patient outcomes are fairly balanced across critical, deceased, improving, and recovered categories, each accounting for roughly 24–26% of cases. This balance suggests that while care is effective for many, there remains a significant proportion of patients in critical or deceased categories, highlighting opportunities for targeted interventions. Overall, the dashboard enables hospital administrators and medical staff to monitor department-specific patient loads and outcomes, supporting data-driven decisions for resource allocation, quality improvement, and patient care optimization.
